Siberian Husky: Dog Breed Profile

Some information about Sibrian Husky.

Source from:

https://www.thesprucepets.com

Breed Overview

  • Group: Working
  • Height: 20 to 23.5 inches at the shoulder
  • Weight: 35 to 60 pounds
  • Affection Level:
  • Energy Level: High
  • Intelligence: Medium
  • Exercise Needs: High
  • Playfulness: High
  • Coat & Color: Huskies have a dense, double coat seen in a variety of colors, including but not limited to combinations of black, gray, white and tan.
    They have markings that include black points, piebald, or pinto.
  • LIFE EXPECTANCY: 12 to 15 years

    • Common Health Problems

    Responsible breeders strive to maintain the highest breed standards as established by kennel clubs like the AKC. Dogs bred by these standards are less likely to inherit health conditions. However, some hereditary health problems can occur in the breed. The following are some conditions to be aware of:
  • Hip Dysplasia: A condition in which the hip socket forms abnormally.
  • Hypothyroidism: A disease where the thyroid doesn't produce a sufficient amount of hormones.
  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy: A group of eye diseases that can eventually lead to blindness.

    • Pros & Cons

    Pros
    1. Friendly and gentle with all ages and most animals
    2. A low predisposition to hereditary diseases
    3. Intelligent and easy to train
    Cons
    1. Heavy shedding, particularly during a twice-a-year shed
    2. Prone to vocalization, including particularly loud howling
    3. Requires a significant amount of exercise
